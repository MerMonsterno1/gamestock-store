• Pac-Man

    The goal is to clear each maze of all pellets while avoiding being caught by the ghosts. "Pac-Man" quickly became an iconic and enduring symbol of video game culture, with its simple yet addictive gameplay and distinctive characters.

    developed by Namco

  • Super Mario Bros

    Players navigate through side-scrolling levels filled with enemies, obstacles, and secrets, using Mario's iconic abilities such as jumping and stomping on enemies to progress. The game introduced many gameplay elements now synonymous with the franchise, including power-ups like the Super Mushroom and Fire Flower, warp zones, and challenging boss battles.

    Nintendo

  • Crash Team Racing

    The game features both single-player modes, such as Adventure mode, where players compete to save the world from the evil alien Nitros Oxide, and multiplayer modes for up to four players. Known for its challenging gameplay, vibrant graphics, and memorable characters, "Crash Team Racing" remains a favorite among fans of the kart racing genre.

    PlayStation
